# OWNER PROJECT BRIEF — CHEN RESIDENCE

**Project:** 14 Wattle Grove, Lindfield NSW 2070
**Owners:** Michael and Sarah Chen
**Date:** 12 May 2026
**Prepared by:** Owners (reviewed with Harrison Clarke Studio)

## Purpose

Replace the existing dwelling with a new family home for long-term occupation. Michael and Sarah have two children (ages 9 and 12) and require space for occasional work-from-home and visiting parents.

## Design objectives

- **Rooms:** 4 bedrooms (main with ensuite), separate study, 2 bathrooms + powder room, open kitchen/living/dining opening to north-facing garden
- **Garage:** Double lock-up with internal access and storage nook
- **Style:** Contemporary coastal — light brick or rendered masonry, timber accents, metal roof; not a period reproduction
- **Levels:** Two storeys acceptable; prefer living on ground where possible
- **Outdoor:** Covered alfresco, level lawn area for kids, room for small vegetable garden
- **Accessibility:** Ground-floor WC and stepless entry preferred (future-proofing)

## Budget

**Working budget ceiling:** $1,850,000 construction cost (excluding landscaping, loose furniture, and authority fees). Owners hold **$120,000 contingency** (approx. 6.5%) for unknowns on sloping/reactive site.

Owners will supply: pendant lights (allowance $8,000), appliances package (allowance $22,000).

## Programme aspirations

- Move out of rental and into new home by **Christmas 2028**
- Accept 18–24 month overall programme if authority and builder timelines require

## Planning assumptions

Owners understand the site is in a heritage conservation area context. They prefer **maximum allowable envelope** rather than minimal footprint. No objection to two-storey built form if council supports.

Owners are **not** assuming CDC at this stage — expect DA process.

## Non-negotiables

- North light to main living
- Bath 1 with bath + shower (kids)
- Laundry internal, not garage-only
- No bedroom on lower level except optional guest/study flex

## Flexibility

Open to architect recommendation on slab vs suspended slab given geotechnical advice. Open on garage orientation if survey/easements constrain.
