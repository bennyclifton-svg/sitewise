## 1. Introduction

**Geotechnical investigation report on file** for Chen Residence footing design (Terratech report TT-GEO-2026-0442).

Two machine-bored boreholes (BH-1 front, BH-2 rear) and two test pits (TP-1, TP-2) were completed to inform footing design for a proposed two-storey dwelling. Survey by North Shore Surveying indicates approx. 2.1 m site fall.

## 2. Site description

Knockdown-rebuild context. Existing single-storey brick cottage on stiffened raft (observed). Natural surface: sandy clay over residual claystone. Groundwater not encountered in boreholes at depths investigated (max 4.5 m).

## 3. Subsurface profile (summary)

| Depth (m) | Material |
| --- | --- |
| 0.0–0.4 | Fill / topsoil |
| 0.4–2.8 | Medium plasticity CLAY, stiff to very stiff, **reactive** |
| 2.8–4.5+ | Weathered claystone, increasing strength |

## 4. Site classification

**AS 2870-2011 Site Classification: H1** (highly reactive clay)

Ys estimated **40–50 mm** (normal moisture conditions). Recommend **stiffened raft slab** or **deepened beams with piers** to refusal in weathered claystone. Conventional strip footings **not recommended** without engineering redesign.

## 5. Key recommendations

1. Structural engineer to adopt H1 parameters; provide footing design to certifier
2. Site drainage critical — maintain moisture equilibrium; no ponding against slab
3. Excavation benching may be required on cut slope at rear; temporary shoring TBC
4. Rock encountered TP-2 at 1.8 m — allow for excavation difficulty in cost plan

## 6. Limitations

Investigation points limited to accessible areas; latent conditions (old footings, fill pockets) may differ locally. Further investigation if unusual excavation reveals undocumented fill.
