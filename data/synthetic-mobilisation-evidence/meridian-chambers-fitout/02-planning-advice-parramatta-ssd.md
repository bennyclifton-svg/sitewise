## Existing use and proposal

## Pathway assessment

| Pathway | Applicability |
| --- | --- |
| Exempt complying development | **Not supported** — works exceed exempt development limits; involve mezzanine structure and egress recalculation |
| CDC | **Not recommended** — mezzanine insert and fire-engineered open plan require performance solution; CDC not available for this scope in current form |
| Local DA (Parramatta Council) | **Available fallback** if SSD refused or deemed unnecessary |
| State Significant Development (SSD) | **Recommended primary pathway** — fit-out value and employment generation threshold likely met; single state assessment preferred for programme certainty |

## Key planning issues

1. **Occupancy load increase** from 98 persons (existing) to **142 persons** proposed — egress and sanitary provision to be demonstrated.
2. **Mezzanine insert** on Level 4 (~185 m²) triggers structural and fire-compartment review.
3. **After-hours works** in partially occupied building — construction management plan and noise/vibration controls will be conditions of consent.
4. **No heritage constraints** — building constructed 1984, not heritage-listed.
5. **NABERS commitment** — landlord lease requires tenant to maintain base building NABERS Energy rating ≥ 4.5 stars; tenant fit-out must not degrade common services efficiency.

## Authority programme

- SSD lodgement target: **September 2026** (subject to fire engineering and architectural package).
- Assessment (SSD): **10–14 weeks** typical.
- Construction certificate (CC): **4–6 weeks** after SSD determination.

## Recommendation

Proceed with **SSD as primary pathway**. Prepare DA fallback package in parallel only if SSD eligibility is challenged. **Do not assume CDC.**

Tenant should obtain written landlord consent to lodge SSD application and carry out after-hours works before builder tender.
