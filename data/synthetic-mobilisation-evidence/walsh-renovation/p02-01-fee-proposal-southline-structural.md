## Project understanding

We understand Atelier North is preparing design development for a late-Victorian terrace renovation, including a first-floor addition over the rear portion, a relocated internal stair, new kitchen/dining opening to the north-facing courtyard, and retention of the front facade and principal rooms.

The existing documents note:

- late-Victorian masonry terrace construction with sandstone plinth;
- live occupation on ground floor where safe and practical;
- party-wall sensitivity at No. 40 and No. 44 Hargrave Street;
- likely latent-condition exposure at the rear lean-to footings;
- DA pathway with heritage-streetscape review.

## Scope of services

1. Initial site inspection and review of existing visible structure.
2. Structural concept advice for DA and design-development coordination.
3. Structural drawings and specifications for construction certificate and tender issue, including:
   - new upper-floor framing;
   - roof framing to the rear addition;
   - steel and LVL beams around the relocated stair opening;
   - rear wall bracing and courtyard opening;
   - preliminary footing details for the rear addition;
   - interface notes for retained masonry and party walls.
4. Tender RFI responses for one head-builder tender round.
5. Three construction-stage inspections: footing, frame, and final structural inspection.

## Assumptions and exclusions

- This proposal is for structural engineering only.
- No destructive opening-up is included before builder strip-out.
- Subsurface investigation, soil classification, and footing-bearing confirmation by others.
- Temporary works design, scaffold design, propping shop drawings, and builder methodology certification are excluded.
- Underpinning design is excluded unless exposed conditions show it is required; if instructed, we will provide a separate fee.
- Asbestos or hazardous-materials identification is by others.
